Daily Highlights

What to see, where to go, and how to get there

Day 1 & 2

Nice & The Riviera

April 23–24 · Thursday–Friday
Promenade des Anglais
An iconic 7 km seaside walk featuring the Belle Époque Hotel Negresco and the Masséna Museum.
Easily accessed from the city center; follow the palm-lined walkway from Castle Hill toward the airport.
Musée Masséna: 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM · Closed Tuesdays
Èze Village & Jardin Exotique
A medieval hilltop village famous for narrow pedestrian streets and artisans. The Jardin Exotique at the summit offers panoramic Mediterranean views.
Park at the Général de Gaulle underground lot. Option: hike the Nietzsche Trail from Èze-sur-Mer (~1 hour).
Garden: daily in April, 9:00 AM – 6:30 PM
Sentier du Littoral (Nice to Villefranche)
A scenic 6 km coastal trail with breathtaking views over the Bay of Villefranche-sur-Mer.
Begin at Jardin Félix Rainaud near "la Réserve" restaurant; follow the stone path along the coast.
Day 3

The High Country

April 25 · Saturday  ·  Nice → Moustiers (150 km)
Route Napoléon
One of France's most scenic drives, winding through the Verdon Pre-Alps with spectacular views of cliffs and pine forests.
Follow the D4085 from Grasse toward Castellane for the most dramatic stretches.
Moustiers-Sainte-Marie
Perched against limestone cliffs, this village is renowned for its 17th-century faïence ceramics and the golden star suspended above the ravine.
Use the designated parking lots on the village outskirts — the center is largely pedestrian.
Day 4

Heart of Provence & Nature

April 26 · Sunday  ·  Moustiers → St. Rémy (117 km)
Luberon Villages – Gordes & Roussillon
Gordes offers a dramatic hilltop silhouette with stone architecture; Roussillon is famous for its vibrant ochre-colored houses.
In Gordes, use the Bel-Air lot for a photo op before parking at the car-free center edge.
Sentier des Ocres (Ochre Trail)
A flat, 45-minute trail through natural ochre quarries with striking red and orange cliffs.
Village of Roussillon; entry fee ~€4.
Saint-Paul de Mausole (St. Rémy)
A relaxed town with artsy streets and the former monastery where Van Gogh resided and painted The Starry Night.
1 km south of town center on Avenue Vincent van Gogh.
Daily 9:30 AM – 7:00 PM · Last entry 6:30 PM
Day 5

Cultural Corridor

April 27 · Monday  ·  St. Rémy → Montpellier (107 km) via Avignon
Palais des Papes – Avignon
A UNESCO World Heritage site and the world's largest Gothic palace, featuring interactive HistoPad tablet tours.
Park at Parking Palais des Papes (Rue Ferruce) directly beneath the monument.
Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM
Jardin des Plantes – Montpellier
Founded in 1593, France's oldest botanical garden with historic greenhouses and medicinal plant collections.
Main entrance on Boulevard Henri IV; 10 min walk from center.
12:00 PM – 6:00 PM · Note: Closed Mondays — if so, explore the historic center instead.
Place de la Comédie & Shopping
The massive pedestrian heart of Montpellier, adjacent to the Polygone Mall — Uniqlo and more.
Follow tram lines 1 or 2 to the "Comédie" stop.
Day 6

Arles & Roman Antiquity

April 28 · Tuesday  ·  Montpellier → Arles (81 km)
Roman Amphitheatre – Les Arènes
A beautifully preserved 1st-century colosseum still used for local festivals.
Central Arles; several paid lots within a 5-minute walk.
Daily 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Fondation Vincent van Gogh Arles
A contemporary art space exploring Van Gogh's legacy through rotating exhibitions alongside modern artists.
35 ter, rue du Docteur-Fanton, near the Rhône river.
Tue–Sun 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM · Last entry 5:30 PM
LUMA Arles
A contemporary arts complex featuring the striking Frank Gehry Tower. Note: Tower and exhibitions are closed on Tuesdays, but the Landscaped Park is open.
Chemin des Minimes
Park open daily 7:00 AM – 6:30 PM · Tower closed Tuesdays
Day 7

Aix-en-Provence

April 29 · Wednesday  ·  Arles → Aix (76 km)
Musée Granet
One of France's finest regional museums, housing a significant collection of works by Paul Cézanne. The Atelier de Cézanne is under renovation until June 2026 — this is the best alternative.
Place Saint-Jean de Malte, a short walk from Cours Mirabeau.
Tue–Sun 12:00 PM – 6:00 PM
Cours Mirabeau
A majestic main boulevard lined with giant plane trees and historic fountains. Perfect for a morning coffee or a Calisson (local almond candy) tasting.
Old Town Exploration
A maze of cobblestone streets and local boutiques, perfect for finding high-quality Provençal crafts.
Day 8

Cassis & The Calanques

April 30 · Thursday  ·  Aix → Cassis (50 km)
Route des Crêtes
A winding cliff-top road (D141) linking Cassis to La Ciotat, offering spectacular viewpoints over the Mediterranean.
Access directly from Cassis; look for the "Sémaphore" viewpoint for the highest vista.
Calanque Port Miou & Port Pin
An easy coastal stroll leading to limestone "fjords" with turquoise waters — the iconic Calanques experience.
Park on Avenue Notre-Dame along Port-Miou; follow the path ~45 min to reach Port Pin.
National Park open 24/7 · Check for fire-hazard closures in hot/windy weather.
